Benefits and Costs of Cross-Listings in the United States

19 Pages Posted: 14 Apr 2021

Tina Nguyen

University of Economics Ho Chi Minh City

Bao Tran Quoc

University of Economics Ho Chi Minh City

Kim Yến Nguyệt Nguyễn

University of Economics Ho Chi Minh City

Lê Hoài Tâm Nguyễn

University of Economics Ho Chi Minh City

Hoàng Vũ Vương

University of Economics Ho Chi Minh City

Date Written: April 26, 2019

Abstract

Cross-listing is the listing of a company’s common shares on a different exchange than its primary and original stock exchange. In order to be approved and listed on other stock exchange, company must meet some requirement in both domestic and foreign country, in accounting and auditing policies.


Keywords: Cross-Listings, United States, ADRs – American Depository Receipts

Suggested Citation:

Nguyen, Tina and Tran Quoc, Bao and Nguyễn, Kim Yến Nguyệt and Nguyễn, Lê Hoài Tâm and Vương, Hoàng Vũ, Benefits and Costs of Cross-Listings in the United States (April 26, 2019). Available at SSRN: https://ssrn.com/abstract=3814180 or http://dx.doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.3814180
