Where and How Do Tax And?Environmental, Social and Governance? Factors Intersect?

European Taxation: Vol. 63 (2023), Issue 12, Pages 554-559

Posted: 22 Nov 2024 Last revised: 18 Feb 2025

Femke van der Zeijden

Independent

H. Myronicheva

Independent

Abstract

In this note, the authors describe the extent to which taxation is reflected in sustainability regulation and frameworks, including the Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation (2019/2088), the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (2022/2464), the Taxonomy Regulation (2020/852), the Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive (2022/71), ESG Rating Regulation (2023/314), the United Nations Principles for Responsible Investment (UNPRI), the OECD Guidelines for Multinational Enterprises on Responsible Business Conduct (OECD RBC), and the UN Sustainable Development Goals and Impact Standards.

Full-text Paper: https://doi.org/10.59403/27cw53n

Keywords: ESG, tax good governance, reporting requirements, disclosure

Suggested Citation:

Zeijden, Femke van der and Myronicheva, H., Where and How Do Tax And?Environmental, Social and Governance? Factors Intersect?. European Taxation: Vol. 63 (2023), Issue 12, Pages 554-559, Available at SSRN: https://ssrn.com/abstract=5028934 or http://dx.doi.org/10.59403/27cw53n
