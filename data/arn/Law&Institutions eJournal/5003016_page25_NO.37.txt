Technology-Enabled Tax Compliance

Bulletin for International Taxation: Vol. 74 (2020), Issue 10, Pages 583-597

Posted: 4 Nov 2024 Last revised: 18 Feb 2025

Bianca Kuijper

Independent

T. Cameron

Independent

Z. Szatmári

Independent

Abstract

This article describes many of the technologies that enable both direct and indirect tax compliance, the recent landmark regulatory events that drive them, and the considerations that tax professionals should take into account before they adopt them.

Full-text Paper: https://doi.org/10.59403/2v58pym

Keywords: information technology, tax technology, tax compliance, direct tax, indirect tax, automation, e-commerce, nexus, MOSS, SAF-T, blockchain

Suggested Citation:

Kuijper, Bianca and Cameron, T. and Szatmári, Z., Technology-Enabled Tax Compliance. Bulletin for International Taxation: Vol. 74 (2020), Issue 10, Pages 583-597, Available at SSRN: https://ssrn.com/abstract=5003016 or http://dx.doi.org/10.59403/2v58pym
