The Ordering of Residence and Source Country Taxes and the OECD Pillar Two Global Minimum Tax

Bulletin for International Taxation: Vol. 76 (2022), Issue 5, Pages 218-227

Posted: 6 Nov 2024 Last revised: 18 Feb 2025

Brian J. Arnold

Independent

Abstract

The author, in this article, examines, by way of examples, the implications in respect of the ordering of residence and source country taxes in relation to the OECD's Pillar Two Global Minimum Tax proposal.

Full-text Paper: https://doi.org/10.59403/p3796g

Keywords: Pillar 2 (OECD), global minimum tax, source principle of taxation, residence principle of taxation, IIR (OECD), top-up tax, UTPR (OECD), effective tax rate, QDMTT (OECD), CFC

Suggested Citation:

Arnold, Brian J., The Ordering of Residence and Source Country Taxes and the OECD Pillar Two Global Minimum Tax. Bulletin for International Taxation: Vol. 76 (2022), Issue 5, Pages 218-227, Available at SSRN: https://ssrn.com/abstract=5003206 or http://dx.doi.org/10.59403/p3796g
