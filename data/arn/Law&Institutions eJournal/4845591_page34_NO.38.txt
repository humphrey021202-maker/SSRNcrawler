Interpreting the Factor of Dominion in Conduit Company Cases

Bulletin for International Taxation. - Amsterdam. - Vol. 73 (2019), no. 5 ; 8 p.; DOI: https://doi.org/10.59403/f2gqp7

Posted: 3 Jun 2024

Saurabh Jain

Independent

John Prebble KC

Victoria University of Wellington, Te Herenga Waka - Faculty of Law; Institut für Österreichisches und Internationales Steuerrecht, Wirtschaftsuniversität Wien; University of Notre Dame Australia - School of Law

Date Written: April 18, 2019

Abstract

The factor of dominion should be interpreted in the light of the object and purpose of double tax treaties. Such an approach in turn helps courts to interpret the beneficial ownership requirement in the same manner as they interpret a general anti-avoidance rule. Certain courts have adopted this approach and come to the correct result.

Full-text Paper

Keywords: conduit company, beneficial ownership, tax treaty, case law

JEL Classification: K33, K34
