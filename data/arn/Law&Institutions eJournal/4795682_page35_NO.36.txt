The Effect of Global Intangible Low-Taxed Income on Us Investment in Australia

Bulletin for International Taxation. - Amsterdam. - Vol. 72 (2018), no. 11 ; p. 651-655; DOI: https://doi.org/10.59403/hyba3g

Posted: 28 May 2024

Anton Joseph

Independent

Date Written: September 20, 2018

Abstract

The author, in this article, considers the implications of the introduction of the concept of global intangible low-taxed income (GILTI) by the US Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017 (TCJA), with its stated intention of encouraging companies to invest in the United States, for US investment in Australia.

Full-text Paper

Keywords: GILTI, foreign investment, CFC, GILTI, conduit company, US Model, double taxation relief, LoB

JEL Classification: K33, K34
