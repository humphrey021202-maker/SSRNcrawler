Small Business Taxation: Revamping Incentives to Encourage Growth

SPP Research Paper No. 4-7

31 Pages Posted: 2 Sep 2011 Last revised: 28 Mar 2013

Jack Mintz

University of Calgary - The School of Public Policy; CESifo (Center for Economic Studies and Ifo Institute)

Duanjie Chen

University of Toronto - Rotman School of Management

Date Written: May 30, 2011

Abstract

This study adopts a new approach in assessing the impact of taxes on small business growth and suggests the need to consider new incentives that would be more effective in encouraging small business growth and would also improve the neutrality of the existing tax system.

Keywords: small business, tax, incentives, economic growth, METR

JEL Classification: H25

Suggested Citation:

Mintz, Jack and Chen, Duanjie, Small Business Taxation: Revamping Incentives to Encourage Growth (May 30, 2011). SPP Research Paper No. 4-7, Available at SSRN: https://ssrn.com/abstract=1920866 or http://dx.doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.1920866
