Tax Opinions & Probability Theory: Lessons from Donald Trump

156 Tax Notes 61, July 3, 2017

15 Pages Posted: 18 Jul 2018

Heather M. Field

UC Law, San Francisco

Date Written: July 3, 2017

Abstract

This report uses tax opinions rendered to Donald Trump’s enterprises in the early 1990s as a case study for examining the relevance of probability theory in multi-issue opinions in which each tax position must be correct for the desired benefits to be achieved. This report also makes recommendations for incorporating probability theory into tax opinion practice.

Keywords: tax opinion, tax law, probability theory, tax ethics, tax practice, professional responsibility

JEL Classification: K34

Suggested Citation:

Field, Heather M., Tax Opinions & Probability Theory: Lessons from Donald Trump (July 3, 2017). 156 Tax Notes 61, July 3, 2017, Available at SSRN: https://ssrn.com/abstract=3209576
