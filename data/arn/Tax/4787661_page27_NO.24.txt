Why No Corporate Tax Inversions to China?

Bulletin for International Taxation. - Amsterdam. - Vol. 71 (2017), no. 7 ; p. 396-404; DOI: https://doi.org/10.59403/204rhd7

Posted: 10 Apr 2024

James G.S. Yang

affiliation not provided to SSRN

Huanyu Ouyang

Independent

Date Written: April 8, 2024

Abstract

Many US corporations have moved abroad, but none have relocated to China. This article reveals that both the United States and China have high tax rates. The United States offers a tax deferral on undistributed foreign earnings, but China does not. China, therefore, has the worst of both worlds.

Full-text Paper

Keywords: corporate inversion, deferral, tax rate, worldwide income

JEL Classification: K33, K34
