Uruguayan Tax Residence and the Tax Holiday: Changes Affecting Individuals

Bulletin for International Taxation: Vol. 75 (2021), Issue 7, Pages 340-346

Posted: 4 Nov 2024 Last revised: 18 Feb 2025

Juan Bonet Tellechea

Independent

C. Valverde

Independent

G. Lorbeer

Independent

Abstract

This article examines the Uruguayan tax residence regime as amended by the most recent changes that apply from 2020 and 2021. It also describes certain key aspects of this special regime and its effect on Uruguay's network of tax treaties.

Full-text Paper: https://doi.org/10.59403/vdwqq5

Keywords: residence, tax holiday, tiebreaker rule, tax treaty, centre of vital interests, habitual abode

Suggested Citation:

Bonet Tellechea, Juan and Valverde, C. and Lorbeer, G., Uruguayan Tax Residence and the Tax Holiday: Changes Affecting Individuals. Bulletin for International Taxation: Vol. 75 (2021), Issue 7, Pages 340-346, Available at SSRN: https://ssrn.com/abstract=5003085 or http://dx.doi.org/10.59403/vdwqq5
