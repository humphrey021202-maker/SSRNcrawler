Is GILTI Constitutional?

Tax Notes Federal, January 11, 2021, p. 283

U of Michigan Law & Econ Research Paper No. 21-018

U of Michigan Public Law Research Paper No. 21-018

6 Pages Posted: 12 Mar 2021 Last revised: 24 Jun 2021

Reuven S. Avi-Yonah

University of Michigan Law School

Date Written: February 6, 2021

Abstract

In this article, Avi-Yonah argues that the global intangible low-taxed income regime may be an unconstitutional attempt to tax the foreign-source income of foreign entities, and he offers an alternative.

Keywords: GILTI, constitutionality

JEL Classification: H26

Suggested Citation:

Avi-Yonah, Reuven S., Is GILTI Constitutional? (February 6, 2021). Tax Notes Federal, January 11, 2021, p. 283, U of Michigan Law & Econ Research Paper No. 21-018, U of Michigan Public Law Research Paper No. 21-018, Available at SSRN: https://ssrn.com/abstract=3780620
