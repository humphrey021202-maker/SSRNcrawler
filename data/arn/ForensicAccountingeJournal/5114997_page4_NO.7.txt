The Impact of Deliberation Time on Ethical Decision Quality: A Study of Early-Career Professional Accountants

https://scholarworks.gsu.edu/bus_admin_diss/149/

https://doi.org/10.57709/22768558

130 Pages Posted: 30 Jan 2025 Last revised: 17 Feb 2025

Ricki Livingston

University of Connecticut - School of Business

Date Written: April 01, 2021

Abstract

This study examines how deliberation time impacts ethical decision-making in early-career professional accountants, particularly in light of disruptive trends like workforce reduction and automation. The findings support the Fraud Triangle Theory, suggesting that workload balancing and ethical awareness are crucial for maintaining ethical decision-making quality.

Keywords: Professional Accounting, Ethics, Fraud Triangle Theory, Decision Quality, Deliberation, Decision Making, Time, and AICPA Code of Professional Conduct
