Deferred Tax of European Publicly Traded Companies in the Automotive Sector Patrik Svoboda

17 Pages Posted: 28 Aug 2025 Last revised: 28 Aug 2025

Patrik Svoboda

Mendel University in Brno

Hana Bohusova

Ambis University

Abstract

This paper examines the significance of deferred tax disclosures and their impact on investor decisions, with a specific focus on publicly traded automotive companies listed on the Frankfurt Stock Exchange. While previous research has predominantly focused on U.S. markets using U.S. GAAP, the European IFRS context, particularly in economically significant sectors such as automotive manufacturing, remains under-researched. This study explicitly addresses this gap by analysing investor perceptions regarding Deferred Tax Assets (DTA) and Deferred Tax Liabilities (DTL), emphasising differences in how investors interpret these two categories. Moreover, this research uniquely examines how the relevance of deferred tax information evolves following the publication of annual financial reports. Empirical results indicate a significant negative relationship between DTAs and stock prices, suggesting that investors are concerned about firms’ future profitability and the realisation of anticipated tax savings. Conversely, DTLs showed no statistically significant impact on stock valuations. Investor reactions were strongest immediately after financial disclosures and gradually diminished over subsequent months.
