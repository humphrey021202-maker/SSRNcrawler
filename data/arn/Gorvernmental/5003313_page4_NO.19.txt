Freedom, Volition and Income Taxes: A Normative Case for the Taxation of Capital Gains on a Realization Basis

Bulletin for International Taxation: Vol. 77 (2023), Issue 10, Pages 434-445

Posted: 6 Nov 2024 Last revised: 18 Feb 2025

Paulo Arthur Cavalcante Koury

Independent

Abstract

This article argues that the right to freedom provides a normative basis for the criterion of realization for the taxation of capital gains. Even though this reason may be surpassed by competing factors in a given legal system, it means that claims that realization has no normative justification are unsound.

Full-text Paper: https://doi.org/10.59403/3a85kmm

Keywords: capital gains tax, constitutional law, income tax, equal treatment

Suggested Citation:
